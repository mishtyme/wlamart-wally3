Executive Summary

Orchestration over Optimization

The Problem

Walmart merchants make 10,000+ connected decisions/week. Decisions are fragmented, optimized locally, and suffer from delayed feedback, causing margin leakage and excess inventory.

The Solution: Merchant Brain

An AI-powered decision orchestration layer. Not just optimizing actions, but evaluating downstream consequences to improve lifecycle P&L while preserving EDLP.

What Makes This Different

  • Orchestration over Optimization: Resolves conflicts across pricing, demand, and inventory.
  • Trust-First Rollout: Read-only → Human-in-the-loop → Guarded automation.
  • Merchants in Control: Overrides are first-class learning signals.

Success Metrics

• Margin recovery
• Inventory aging reduction
• Merchant time saved
• Override & trust metrics

Execution Strategy

A Trust-First Approach

Weeks 0-8 Phase 0

Merchant Shadowing & Deep Discovery

Build trust before building software. Understand real heuristics vs documented process.

Key Activity Embedded shadowing in pricing resets & promo planning.
Deliverable Decision taxonomy & Trust Map.
Weeks 9-16 Phase 1

Read-Only Decision Intelligence

Focus only on non-reversible, advisory signals. No write access. No automation.

Scope Markdown alerts, Demand anomaly detection.
Success Metric Signal precision & Merchant agreement rate.
Weeks 17-28 Phase 2

Human-in-the-Loop Recommendations

Recommendations include actions, P&L impact, and confidence scores. Overrides capture "why".

Workflow Accept, Modify, or Reject (with reason code).
Goal Generate training data from overrides.
Weeks 29-44 Phase 3

Limited Auto-Execution

Automation governed by hard business rules, not just model confidence.

Guardrails Never violate EDLP. Never execute irreversible actions without confirmation.
Target Low-risk, high-confidence, low-dollar impact decisions.

Top Challenges

Data Fragmentation

Fragmented sources. Strategy: Surface uncertainty rather than waiting for perfect data.

Label Scarcity

Lack of "good" vs "bad" labels. Strategy: Treat overrides as first-class training data.

Merchant Skepticism

Strategy: Optimize for voluntary usage first. Let adoption lag metrics.

Failure Modes & Rollback

Immediate Rollback

Confidence threshold breach disables auto-exec instantly.

Kill Switches

Global, Category, and Merchant-level kill switches available.

"Failure is treated as expected input, not exception."
