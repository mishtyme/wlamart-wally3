1. Executive Summary

The Problem

Walmart merchants operate in a high-stakes environment making 10,000+ interconnected decisions per week. These decisions are currently fragmented, locally optimized, and delayed—leading to margin leakage and cognitive overload.

The Proposal: Merchant Brain

An AI-powered orchestration layer. Instead of isolated optimizations, it evaluates downstream consequences to recommend or safely automate decisions that improve lifecycle P&L while protecting EDLP.

Orchestration

Resolves conflicts across pricing, demand, and inventory.

Trust-First

Read-only → HITL → Guarded automation rollout.

Control

Merchant overrides are first-class learning signals.

Safety

Built-in rollback, kill-switches, and confidence decay.

2. The Core Problem

The Merchant's Impossible Job (Why Now at Walmart)

Walmart-Specific Realities

EDLP is Non-Negotiable

We cannot rely on reactive price swings. We must protect trust while managing cost volatility.

Peak Risks

Holiday/Back-to-School compress timelines. Mistakes here are highly visible and costly.

Asymmetric Costs

Over-discounting permanently erodes margin; under-reacting causes aged inventory.

2.1 Decision Overload & Fragmentation

  • Decisions made in isolation across disconnected tools
  • Feedback arrives weeks late (systemic margin leakage)
  • Merchants spend ~80% of time reacting, not optimizing

4.1 The 6-Agent Model (Click to expand)

Assortment Agent

What to carry and what to exit based on category goals.

Role: Evaluates performance to recommend Adds/Deletes.

Demand Agent

Forecasts how much to buy and when.

Role: Ingests sales trends, seasonality, and external signals.

Pricing Agent

Base price, adjustments, markdown timing.

Role: Optimizes elasticity while adhering to EDLP guardrails.

Promotion Agent

When and how deeply to promote.

Role: Balances traffic driving vs margin erosion; checks cannibalization.

Supply Agent

Replenishment, lead time, supplier constraints.

Role: Ensures availability and optimizes logistics costs.

Liquidation Agent

Early markdowns, clearance routing, exits.

Role: Minimizes loss on end-of-life inventory.

Read-Only Decision Intelligence

Advisory Only

Signal Quality Metrics:
Precision@K, False positive rate.
Merchant Behavior Metrics:
Alert open rate, Dismissal rate.
2

Human-in-the-Loop

Weeks 17–28

Partnership

Recommendations include Suggested Action, P&L Impact, and Confidence Score.

  • Accept
  • Modify
  • Reject (w/ Reason)

Limited Auto-Execution

Automation

Governed by hard business rules (Deterministic Guardrails), not just model confidence.

11. Challenges & Tradeoffs

Explicit Hard Decisions

Data Fragmentation

Tradeoff: Perfect vs Usable

Signals span POS, inventory, supplier feeds with varying quality.

"Ship with uncertainty surfaced rather than wait for data perfection."

Label Scarcity

Tradeoff: Purity vs Learning

Historical data lacks clean "good/bad" labels.

"Treat merchant overrides as first-class training data."

Compute Cost vs Value

Tradeoff: Precision vs Cost

Large-scale simulations are expensive.

"Spend compute where merchants spend judgment (High impact SKUs)."

Automation Risk

Tradeoff: Speed vs Safety

Trust collapses easily with automation errors.

"Immediate rollback on confidence breaches."

12. Failure, Rollback & Metrics

Rollback Playbook

Immediate (Automated) Confidence threshold breach disables auto-exec instantly.
Short-Term (Human) Merchant-initiated rollback within SLA.
Systemic Model freeze, Root-cause analysis, Re-simulation.

Metrics Framework (Non-North-Star)

Financial
  • Gross margin lift
  • Markdown recovery
  • Inventory turns
Operational
  • Decision cycle time
  • Workload reduction
Trust & Adoption
  • Override trends
  • Repeat usage
  • Opt-out rates
